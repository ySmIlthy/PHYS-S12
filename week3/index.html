Eletronics & Tools

This week’s task consisted in creating a kinetic sculpture, essentially an art
piece with movement attached to its presentation. My inspiration for this assignment
was a sculpture designed by Jonathan Prince (@jonathanprincesculpture) named “Cauldron”.
The sculpture is made out of Marine Grade Stainless Steel and its top shape resembles
the movement of ocean waves.

_____________________________________________________________________________

With this idea in mind, I decided to create an ocean wave simulator,
using an arduino board and a motor. My first step was the planning and brainstorming,
which later guided me to sketch a simple triangular rod with circular like projections
on its foundation. The spinning of this mechanism would then create wave-like movement
on the surface above.

_____________________________________________________________________________

I started by designing the support which connects the wood sticks together and makes a
solid foundation for the rod to spin. This required some measurements to be taken, basic
Fusion360 skills, and knowledge in laser cutting. The creation of a box was also required
and for this matter I decided to use the website Makercase, which basically does all th
dirty work for you in a couple clicks. Upon designing and testing all these pieces, they turned out
looking better than anticipated.

_____________________________________________________________________________

Assembling it all together was the next step, I was required to use a drill to carve holes
in the box and the edges of the mechanism so the setup could be concluded. Once it was all put
together, I layed down a thin blue tissue paper on the top of the box so movement could be seen.
The arduino setup was simple and straightforward. Nothing more than a couple wires and some lines of code.
The mechanism turned out working well and no malfunctions happened.
