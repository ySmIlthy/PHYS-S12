3D Designing & Printing

Something really interesting came up and I was able to utilize my abilities of 3d printing to help
other peopleâ€™s projects. Essentially, for my other class, some of my peers had a device in which they
needed a stable way to adjust some sensors and lasers, so they could run some experiments. We call this
device Maglav and this will be the walkthrough of how I did its cover.

_____________________________________________________________________________

Most, if not all the work, was designed in Fusion360. For my sketch, nothing more than a ruler was used
to take the measurements, which I thought would turn out as an issue, however it was not really the case.
Basically, I replicated the measurements I took and expanded them, so it would create a sort of case for the device.
I separated it into 2 files, respectively, the bottom of it and the actual case that would go on top and has the
designed space for the sensors.

_____________________________________________________________________________

My biggest challenge was to make the software of the Dremel printer work, but after some clicks here and there
I was able to download it and set up the configurations for it. I then exported the model I had in Fusion360 to
Dremel software and everything from this point was just setting it up and changing the specifics for that print,
such as filament density, speed of the print, and balancing of the print board.

_____________________________________________________________________________

The device turned out to be a perfect fit, yet I honestly think that it is way too tight for comfort, yet
they needed it as soon as possible so they could start running the experiments so they just stuck with it.
