3D Scanning

This time I will be talking about my experience with 3d scanning and how it was my first time using
RevoPoint for scanning. I was free to select whatever type of object and I ended up trying 3 different
ones, yet only documented the one that worked. From the three, the dinosaur was the one that worked
and the failed ones were the measuring tape and pair of glasses.

I pretty much followed the steps presented to me during the lab. I started by using the RevoPoint
scan and the rotation table to make the scan of the dinosaur, as shown below.

_____________________________________________________________________________

After the scan was concluded, the compiling and meshing of the structure scanned follows the process.
I saved the file as stl and proceeded to transfer it to the prusa slicer.

_____________________________________________________________________________

Once in the prusa slicer, it is just a matter of waiting for the software to do its thing and finish
it by saving in a SD Card and taking it to the prusa printer.

_____________________________________________________________________________

The print took 17 minutes and came out looking really tiny. Overall, I would say that the scanner is
good for clean surfaces, but not necessarily for every kind of structure. I also felt that there is a
lot of bouncing back and forth between softwares, which is something that I honestly do not like.
